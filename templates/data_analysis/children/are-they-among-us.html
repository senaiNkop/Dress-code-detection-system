    This research analyzes the shootings that are taking place in New York critically and aims to uncover the shooters, motivations, and affiliations of the victims.

    The NYPD Shooting Incident Data (Historical), which covers the years 2006 to 2020, is the dataset used for the projects. Click here to obtain the dataset and learn more about how the data was gathered. Only 42% of the shooters in the tens of thousands of shootings that have taken place in New York alone have been identified; the other shooters are still at large.

    For context, if there are 1000 shootings in New York in a year, 420 gunmen are apprehended by police, while 580 remain at large. This indicates that there is a roughly 60% possibility of the shooters eluding capture.

    To begin our journey of discovering the identities of our shooters. I started by identifying the sex of the shooters and their victims. Which showed that 1 out of 40 shooters are female, and also 1 out of 10 victims are FEMALE. To put it simply, it means that while there are a whole lot fewer shooters that are female, there are significantly more female victims.

    But despite the fact that men make up the majority of the shooters, MANY OF THEIR VICTIMS APPEAR TO BE WOMEN. I tried to determine the reasons why the crimes were being committed by concentrating primarily on the female victims. Why Do They Have to Suffer for our Actions?

    From the information above, we are beginning to identify the shooters and have understanding of their crimes.

    • The majority of the shooters are males
    • Most of the shootings occurs in homes

    The shooters were further categorized into age groups, which led to the discovery that majority of the the women were shot by men their own age

    The analysis from the above plot indicates that:

    For victims who lived in Multi-dwelling apartments:

    • The majority of the shooters falls with the age group of 25–44
    • Shooters between the ages of 18 and 24 perpetrate the majority of crimes against those under the age of 18 and above the age of 65. Which are more likely to be their siblings, parents or grandparents.
    • Shooters between the age of 25–44 perpetrate the majority of crimes against those between the age of 18–64. Which may likely be their siblings, partners or spouse.

    Note: The nature of the house must also be taken into account;

    Since it is a multi-dwell flat, victims and perpetrators under 24 are more likely to live with their parents and siblings rather than possess their own apartment. Because of this, the age group for victims attacked by shooters between 18–24 falls with less than 18 years and higher than 65 years.

    The analysis only applies to those who lives in Multi-dwell apt, and also does neglect to mention that some of these shooters might be complete strangers unaffiliated with their victims.

    These demonstrates that the majority of shooters who target women do so because they are people who are either familiar with the victims or have access to their home. A member of this group might be a friend, a spouse, a a sibling, or even a parent.
